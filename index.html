About

Research Overview

I am a researcher and engineer with 6+ years of experience across autonomous vehicles, robotic perception, sensor fusion, motion planning, and embedded systems. My research bridges the gap between deep learning theory and real-world deployment on physical robotic platforms.

My doctoral work at Clemson University focused on vision-based navigation for autonomous vehicles and cybersecurity for connected vehicle infrastructure. Since then, I have worked across the full AV stack at General Motors R&D and Defense, MicroVision, and Imec USA R&D — filing 18+ patents and publishing in IEEE T-ITS, IEEE TVT, IEEE Access, and top transportation venues.

I am passionate about STEM education and have designed K-12 courses in Python programming, autonomous vehicle simulation, and robotics — supported by the U.S. DOT Center for Connected Multimodal Mobility.

Industry Simu5G Integration into GM R&D Connected and Autonomous Vehicle Simulation Stack
$200K
Principal Investigator Internal Funding · GM R&D
Led and secured internal research funding to integrate the Simu5G tool — a system-level 5G NR and LTE network simulator — into General Motors R&D's connected and autonomous vehicle simulation stack. The project established a 5G communication network within the AV simulation environment and evaluated 5G communication performance for connected vehicle infrastructure applications. Responsible for full project leadership from proposal through execution and delivery.
Collaborating Partners: GM R&D China · University of Pisa (Dr. Giovanni Nardini, Dr. Antonio Virdis) · GM R&D Engineering Counterparts
Lunar Mobility Vehicle — Autonomous Perception & Navigation System
~$5M
Technical Lead · Perception & Navigation GM R&D · Industry Partnership
Supported and led the technical aspects of the Lunar Mobility Vehicle (LMV) program — a $5M project at General Motors R&D in collaboration with Lunar Outpost. Responsibilities included developing deep learning-based perception algorithms, sensor fusion pipelines, and autonomous navigation systems designed to operate reliably in the extreme and unstructured environment of the lunar surface. Work addressed unique challenges of lunar terrain perception including dust, low-light conditions, and the absence of GPS-based localization.
Collaborating Partner: Lunar Outpost · General Motors R&D

Quantum Security for Blockchain-Based VANETs
Collaborative research investigating the implications of quantum computing on the security of blockchain-based Vehicular Ad-Hoc Networks (VANETs). The study demonstrates how quantum Shor's Algorithm can break traditional cryptographic techniques used in VANETs, exposes critical vulnerabilities in existing blockchain security frameworks, and proposes post-quantum countermeasures to protect V2X communications against emerging quantum threats. Published in IEEE Transactions on Intelligent Transportation Systems (Impact Factor: 7.9).
